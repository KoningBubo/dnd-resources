#### Magical Chessmen of Ultham-Urre: Green Pawn
*Wondrous Item, Artifact*
:
![magical chessmen of ultham-urre: green pawn](https://github.com/KoningBubo/dnd-resources/blob/main/images/items/magical_items/artifact/magical_chessmen_of_ultham-urre_green_pawn.png?raw=true) {position:absolute,top:0px,left:470px,width:270px}
This artifact is part of a chessboard complete with 32 chess pieces with one set being red and the other being green representing people from Jasyntha.

:

***Energy Resistance.*** Holding the chess piece in your hand grants you resistance to lightning damage.

:

***Command Word Transform (free action).*** By speaking this command word you are instantly transformed into the representation of the piece, which in this case is a low-level fighter. While in this form you lose all your abilities but gain the abilities, equipment and levels of the representation of this piece.

The new form always starts at full hit points and ends either after an hour has passed, you say the command word again or if you drop to 0 hit points. Any injuries you sustain in this form will be completely healed up after transforming back. Carry-over damage you sustain in this form after dropping to zero hit points will be carried over to your original form.

This ability recharges after 24 hours.

:

***Command Word Bag of Holding (action).*** Speaking this command word opens a small portal the size of your hand that functions as a Bag of Holding.

Taking things out of the portal can be tricky though. Instead of retrieving what you want, roll to determine which item comes out.

:

***Magical Magnetism.*** The chess piece is attracted to metal imbued with magic. Roll 1d100 at the end of each session. If you roll a 1, the chess piece will have teleported away from your person to any magical metal that was in the area.

If during the session you spent time in a city, you roll with disadvantage. If you’ve only spent time in a barely populated area such as in a forest or at sea, you roll with advantage.
